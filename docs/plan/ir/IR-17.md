# IR-17 — Durable step execution + resume

## Purpose

A flow interrupted at step 4 of 7 resumes from step 5 with steps 1–4's results restored, instead of the whole flow being all-or-nothing. Per-step state persists across process death.

## BubbleLab today (the problem)

The open-core hooks exist as stubs: `BubbleRunner.runStep()` (`packages/bubble-runtime/src/runtime/BubbleRunner.ts:396`) and `resumeFromStep()` (:756) are `// @ts-expect-error - Not implemented` methods with `// No implementation` bodies. Real durability lives only in BubbleLab's proprietary platform. In open-core, a flow that dies at step 4 re-runs from step 1, re-executing side effects (emails re-sent, records re-created) or fails outright. The pieces a resume needs do exist nearby: `BaseBubble` already carries `previousResult` with a short-circuit in `action()` (return the saved result without calling `performAction`, `base-bubble-class.ts:267`), which is a per-run memoization mechanism with no persistence behind it.

## The delta

Implement real per-step state and resume: persist each step's result keyed by call-site identity (which `BubbleParser` already assigns), and on resume, rehydrate completed steps' `previousResult` so the existing short-circuit replays them for free while execution proceeds from the first incomplete step. This is filling a hole they left open, not fighting their design; the stubs and the memoization seam are the design, unimplemented.

## Why the improvement is needed

Correctness and cost. All-or-nothing flows make transient failures expensive exactly in proportion to how much the flow already did: a network blip after 3 writes forces either re-running the writes (duplicate side effects, the worst failure mode for an automation product) or abandoning the run. Durability also underpins the sign-off gate's audit story (IR-9/10) and long-running flows generally; without it, every improvement upstream still ships on an execution model that cannot survive a restart.

## Reference build vs BubbleLab port

In `integration_stitcher` durable resume was built into the executor and verified directly: an interrupted flow resumes from the last completed step without re-running it.

In BubbleLab this is the low-risk surgery of the pile: the method stubs, the `previousResult` short-circuit, and call-site identity are all present, so the port adds a persistence layer and a resume orchestration rather than replacing a working mechanism. What fights: the temp-file execution model (a resumed run re-imports and re-executes the script from the top, relying on memoized short-circuits rather than a true program counter), and result payloads must be serializable to persist, which large or non-JSON results may violate.

## Evidence / status

CONFIRMED in the reference build: an interrupted flow resumes from the last completed step without re-running it. Port status: not scheduled (status map: SURGERY, deferred with IR-15/16).

## Verdict
